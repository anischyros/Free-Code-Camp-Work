Skills and interests

A programming geek and full-stack web developer

Bob is a very experienced coder, with expertise in with:

  • C/C++
  • Java, AXIS, Tomcat, JSP and servlets, and Swing/AWT
  • C#, ASP.NET
  • HTML5, JavaScript, JQuery, JQuery UI, and Bootcamp
  • Groovy, Grails, GSP and groovlets
There's more but let's not brag about it.

Technical writer and ghostwriter

Bob has ghostwritten one book on the types of metadata used in information systems found in libraries, museums, and archives. He is also currently writing a book on open-source integrated library systems.

Amateur legal scholar and researcher

Bob learned a great deal when he was forced to fight a battle with the government over certain constitutional issues, one which he won. He learned from that experience that he has the legal aptitude to have excelled in law school, as well as a great deal about how the courts work and constitutional law.

History wonk, particularly of computer history

Bob loves history — any kind of history — and has a particular fondness for Mark Twain and Winston Churchill.

Inveterate diarist and letter-writer

Bob's has been keeping a diary — or is it a journal, a commonplace book, or all of the above — for many years, and it keeps him sane. He also has many correspondents and regularly writes letters, on paper and with a fountain pen, that are then sent via snail mail. Many of his correspondents are prisoners, people who need to receive mail to know that there is life on the other side of the razor wire.

Propeller head

Bob loves reading and learning about new technology and new breakthroughs in science and technology. He is not an early adopter, however, preferring to read about new things and only adopt when it becomes something that he can use.
